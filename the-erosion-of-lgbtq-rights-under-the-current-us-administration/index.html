The Erosion of LGBTQ+ Rights Under the Current U.S. Administration

1 March 2025 LGBTQ+
 
In recent years, LGBTQ+ rights in the United States have seen significant advancements, with legal protections expanding to ensure equality for all citizens regardless of gender identity or sexual orientation. However, the current administration has taken steps that threaten to roll back these hard-won gains, raising concerns among human rights advocates and LGBTQ+ communities.

Executive Order 14168: A Step Backwards

One of the most alarming actions taken by the administration is Executive Order 14168, which redefines sex and gender in federal policies as strictly binary. This order has far-reaching consequences, effectively erasing recognition and protections for transgender and non-binary individuals.

The effects of this executive order include:

  • Restrictions on Legal Identity Changes: Transgender individuals can no longer update their gender markers on federal documents, such as passports and Social Security records. This not only forces them to carry identification that does not reflect their true gender but also exposes them to potential discrimination and harassment when presenting these documents. Incorrect legal identification can create barriers to employment, travel, and accessing government services, making daily life significantly more difficult for transgender individuals.

  • Ban on Transgender Military Service: The order reinstates the ban on transgender individuals serving in the U.S. military, reversing previous policies that allowed them to serve openly. This action disregards the contributions of thousands of transgender service members and denies individuals the right to serve their country based on their gender identity. Studies, including those conducted by the RAND Corporation, have shown that allowing transgender individuals to serve openly does not negatively impact military readiness or unit cohesion. By enforcing this ban, the administration not only discriminates against a segment of the population but also undermines the principles of equality within the armed forces.

  • Healthcare Barriers: Access to gender-affirming healthcare, especially for minors, is being severely restricted or outright banned in multiple states, limiting essential medical care. Gender-affirming treatments, such as hormone therapy and puberty blockers, have been recognized by major medical organizations, including the American Medical Association and the American Academy of Pediatrics, as necessary and life-saving for many transgender individuals. Denying access to these treatments contributes to higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide among transgender youth. Additionally, some states have introduced legislation that criminalizes healthcare providers who offer gender-affirming care, further discouraging medical professionals from providing essential services.

These policy changes mark a stark reversal from previous efforts to ensure equal rights and protections for LGBTQ+ individuals in public life.

The Impact on the LGBTQ+ Community

The rollback of rights and protections has immediate and long-term consequences for LGBTQ+ individuals, particularly transgender and non-binary people.

  1. Legal and Social Marginalization By enforcing a rigid definition of gender, the administration is legally undermining the identities of transgender and non-binary individuals. This increases the risk of discrimination in employment, housing, and public accommodations. Without legal recognition, many transgender individuals may find it harder to secure stable employment, rent housing, or receive fair treatment in institutions like schools and hospitals. The erosion of protections opens the door for discriminatory practices to become more widespread and normalized.

  2. Mental and Physical Health Risks The restriction of gender-affirming healthcare can lead to serious mental health challenges, including higher rates of depression, anxiety, and suicide among transgender youth. Studies have shown that access to gender-affirming care significantly improves mental health outcomes for transgender individuals. When states restrict such care, transgender people are left with few options, often forcing them to travel long distances or seek black-market alternatives, which can be dangerous and unreliable. The cumulative stress caused by these barriers contributes to worsening physical and mental health conditions.

  3. Encouragement of Discriminatory Practices Government policies influence societal attitudes. By actively restricting LGBTQ+ rights, the administration emboldens discriminatory behavior in workplaces, schools, and public institutions, leading to increased hate crimes and violence against LGBTQ+ individuals. When discriminatory policies are enacted at the federal level, they set a precedent for private businesses, schools, and other institutions to follow suit, leading to an environment where discrimination is more acceptable and prevalent.

A Call to Action

The erosion of LGBTQ+ rights under the current administration highlights the importance of continued advocacy and civic engagement. Here’s how individuals and organizations can take action:

  • Stay Informed: Follow updates on policies that affect LGBTQ+ rights through reputable sources:

    • Human Rights Campaign (HRC)

    • American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU)

    • National Center for Transgender Equality

    • GLAAD

    • Lambda Legal

  • Support LGBTQ+ Organizations: Donate to and volunteer with organizations that protect LGBTQ+ rights:

    • Trevor Project – Crisis intervention and suicide prevention for LGBTQ+ youth.

    • OutRight Action International – Global advocacy for LGBTQ+ human rights.

    • Trans Lifeline – Peer support and microgrants for trans individuals.

  • Vote and Advocate: Support candidates and policies that protect LGBTQ+ rights in local, state, and national elections:

    • Rock the Vote – Find voter registration and election info.

    • Ballot Ready – Research candidates and policies.

  • Speak Out: Use social media, petitions, and public forums to raise awareness and push back against regressive policies:

    • Change.org – Start and sign petitions for LGBTQ+ rights.

    • MoveOn – Mobilization for progressive change.

    • ActBlue – Support progressive causes financially.

The fight for LGBTQ+ equality is far from over. While recent policies pose significant challenges, collective action and continued resistance can help ensure that progress is not lost. Human rights are not negotiable, and it is up to all of us to safeguard them for future generations.
