The Dangers of Environmental Deregulation and Its Long-Term Consequences

1 March 2025 Environmental Deregulation

Environmental protections play a crucial role in safeguarding public health, preserving natural ecosystems, and mitigating climate change. However, recent administrative actions have rolled back key environmental regulations, raising significant concerns among scientists, environmental advocates, and public health experts. These efforts include appointing industry lobbyists to the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), declaring a national energy emergency to fast-track fossil fuel extraction, and initiating reviews to challenge the scientific consensus on greenhouse gas emissions.

This article examines the impact of these policies and explores what they mean for the future of environmental sustainability, public health, and climate action.

1. Appointing Industry Lobbyists to Key Positions Within the EPA

One of the most controversial decisions made by the administration has been the appointment of industry lobbyists to senior roles within the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). This move raises concerns about regulatory capture, where government agencies are influenced by the very industries they are supposed to regulate.

  • Conflicts of Interest: Many of these appointees have previously worked for oil, gas, and coal companies, creating an inherent conflict of interest. Their influence within the EPA has led to weakened environmental standards and reduced enforcement of pollution controls.

  • Deregulation of Pollutants: Key environmental safeguards have been weakened, including regulations on air and water pollution, toxic chemicals, and industrial waste disposal. This rollback has resulted in increased pollution, disproportionately impacting low-income communities and marginalized groups.

By placing corporate interests ahead of public health and environmental protection, these appointments threaten the EPA’s mission to safeguard clean air, water, and public lands.

Related Resource: Union of Concerned Scientists – Political Influence in the EPA

2. Declaring a National Energy Emergency to Expedite Fossil Fuel Extraction

In an effort to increase domestic fossil fuel production, the administration has declared a national energy emergency. This declaration expedites the approval of new oil and gas drilling projects, reduces environmental review processes, and prioritizes fossil fuel development over renewable energy investments.

  • Environmental Impact: Expanding fossil fuel extraction leads to increased carbon emissions, deforestation, and habitat destruction. Oil spills, methane leaks, and groundwater contamination are among the long-term consequences of this aggressive push for energy production.

  • Public Health Consequences: Burning fossil fuels releases harmful pollutants, including sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulate matter. These pollutants contribute to respiratory diseases, cardiovascular problems, and premature deaths, particularly in communities located near industrial sites.

By bypassing critical environmental regulations in the name of energy security, this policy undermines efforts to transition toward cleaner and more sustainable energy sources.

Related Resource: Environmental Defense Fund – Impacts of Fossil Fuel Expansion

3. Initiating Reviews to Challenge the Scientific Consensus on Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Another alarming aspect of the administration’s environmental policy is its effort to question the scientific consensus on greenhouse gas emissions and climate change. These efforts include ordering reviews of climate reports, promoting misleading information about carbon emissions, and weakening regulatory frameworks that address climate change.

  • Ignoring Scientific Evidence: The overwhelming majority of climate scientists agree that human activities—primarily the burning of fossil fuels—are the leading cause of global warming. By downplaying or misrepresenting scientific findings, the administration is delaying crucial action on climate change.

  • Dismantling Climate Policies: Environmental policies such as emissions standards for vehicles and power plants have been weakened or revoked. Rollbacks on these regulations contribute to higher levels of carbon pollution, accelerating global temperature rise and extreme weather events.

Efforts to cast doubt on climate science not only stall policy action but also mislead the public about the urgency of the climate crisis.

Call to Action: What Can Be Done?

While these policy rollbacks present significant challenges, there are still ways for individuals and organizations to push back and advocate for stronger environmental protections. Here’s how you can take action:

1. Support Environmental Advocacy Organizations

Many organizations are actively fighting against environmental deregulation and advocating for sustainable policies. Supporting them through donations, volunteering, or spreading awareness can make a real difference:

  • Natural Resources Defense Council (NRDC) – Advocates for policies to combat climate change and protect the environment.

  • Sierra Club – Focuses on clean energy, land conservation, and environmental justice.

  • Greenpeace USA – Campaigns against environmental destruction and promotes sustainability.

2. Stay Informed and Engage in Policy Advocacy

Being informed about environmental issues is the first step to making a difference. Consider using these reliable sources to stay updated:

  • Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) – Official environmental regulations and policy changes.

  • The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) – Scientific assessments on climate change.

  • Earthjustice – Legal advocacy for environmental protection.

3. Vote for Leaders Who Prioritize Climate Action

Elected officials play a critical role in shaping environmental policies. Voting for candidates who support sustainability and strong climate regulations is essential:

  • League of Conservation Voters – Tracks politicians' environmental voting records.

  • Rock the Vote – Helps young people register to vote and participate in elections.

  • Ballot Ready – Provides nonpartisan information about candidates and policies.

4. Reduce Your Carbon Footprint

Individual actions matter. Making environmentally conscious choices can help reduce carbon emissions:

  • Switch to renewable energy providers where possible.

  • Reduce reliance on single-use plastics and adopt sustainable products.

  • Support businesses that prioritize sustainability.

Conclusion

Environmental deregulation under the current administration poses severe risks to both public health and climate stability. By appointing industry insiders to regulatory positions, expediting fossil fuel extraction, and challenging established climate science, these policies undermine decades of environmental progress. The long-term consequences will not only affect ecosystems and biodiversity but will also jeopardize future generations' ability to thrive in a stable climate.

However, the power to drive change lies with the public. Supporting environmental advocacy groups, staying informed, voting for leaders committed to sustainability, and making greener choices in daily life can collectively counteract these rollbacks. Climate action is a shared responsibility—our future depends on the steps we take today.
